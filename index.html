I work at the intersection of fisheries science, climate change and coastal communities, almost always in close collaboration with fishermen, agencies and interdisciplinary research teams rather than in isolation.

Areas of Focus

Fisheries Socioeconomics
Resilience in the Gulf of Maine Lobster Fishery

Building socioeconomic indicators — from landings and crew data to coastal housing access — that track how Maine's lobster fleet and communities are weathering change, and feeding them directly into stock assessment and management.

Marine Policy '25 ICES J. Mar. Sci. '25
Climate Adaptation
Fisheries in a Changing Climate

Studying how marine heatwaves and shifting species ranges alter fishing behavior and yield, and how marine protected area design can adapt to climate-driven change.

Marine heatwave paper (in review) One Earth '23
Ocean & Food Policy
Global Food Systems

Connecting small-scale fisheries to broader questions of food security, equity and vulnerability — from COVID-19 shocks to global market systems to the right to safe food amid climate change.
